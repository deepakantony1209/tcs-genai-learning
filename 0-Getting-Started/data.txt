It is quite interesting and rooted in a great historical context. Our parliamentary procedures are similar to those of the Parliament in England. Even during the era of monarchy, a parliament existed there. At that time, there was a King who held all powers, and there was also a functioning Parliament. Often, the King would wish to carry out a certain task, but the Parliament would reject his proposal. The person tasked with conveying this rejection to the King was the 'Speaker' of the Parliament.

In those days, the King had the authority to sentence any citizen to death. If he learned that the Parliament had blocked his wishes, he would order the execution of the messenger. He would command, 'Behead him!' Consequently, no one wanted to take up the post of the Speaker.

Therefore, in tradition, whenever a new assembly was formed and a Speaker was announced, that person would immediately try to run away to save their life. Then, the Leader of the House and the Leader of the Opposition would literally pull him back and force him to sit in the Chair. This is the tradition that has been followed since those times.
